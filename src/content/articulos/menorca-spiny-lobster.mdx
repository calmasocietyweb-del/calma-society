It is Menorca's most international product and, at the same time, one of the most misunderstood: it is bought all summer long, but it is truly fished only **five months a year**. The red spiny lobster —*Palinurus elephas*, the Mediterranean spiny lobster, which here we call *llagosta*— is a luxury with a calendar, a rulebook and a trick for not being fooled.

This article tells what almost no one explains on the menu: when there is Menorcan lobster, how it is fished, where the best lives and, above all, **how to know that the one they serve you is from here**.

## The essentials

- **Catch season: 1 April to 31 August.** The rest of the year —some seven months— there is a **closure** in Balearic waters.
- **It is fished with the *nansa*** (a conical pot) and the trammel net; the rules forbid setting both gears at once and limit the size of the pot (max. 90 × 60 cm).
- **The best grounds are the North of Menorca Marine Reserve:** 5,119 hectares between Fornells and Cap Gros, created in 1999, where trawling, purse seining and longlining are banned.
- **Traceability:** every legal lobster is marked with a **coloured band according to the guild**; it is the way to prove it is from Menorca. *(Note: there is no registered designation-of-origin seal; the documented individual yellow tag belongs to Ibiza, not Menorca.)*
- **Prices and catches change every year:** in 2025, the first sale ran to around **€70–75/kg**, with some **17,950 kg** landed (less than in 2024). Check the current season.

## A strict calendar

Menorcan lobster may be fished only **from 1 April to 31 August**. Outside that window, the law protects the species during spawning and growth. This has a practical consequence worth memorising: **a local, fresh lobster in October does not exist**. Whatever is served then will be from a holding tank, frozen or imported —perfectly legal if declared, but not the same thing.

The traditional gear is the *nansa*, a funnel-shaped trap set on the rocky bottom, and the trammel net. Balearic regulation keeps the balance: the two gears cannot be used at the same time, and the pot has a maximum size. It is a fishery of patience, of small-scale gear —the opposite of the industrial kind.

## Where the best lives

The ground par excellence is the **North of Menorca Marine Reserve**, 5,119 hectares running from the bay of Fornells to Cap Gros. Created in **1999**, it turned 25 in 2024, and within it the most aggressive gears are banned: the result is a healthy seabed that acts as the Mediterranean's larder and nursery. Lobster is also fished in the **inner waters of the south**, between Cap d'Artrutx and l'Illa de l'Aire. That the Consell de Menorca has a say over whether boats from other islands may work here gives the measure of what is being protected.

## How to know it is from Menorca

Here is the key to the article. Legal lobster caught in the Balearics is **marked with a coloured band or tag according to the guild** of origin and travels with its traceability paperwork. It is your right **to ask for that guarantee tag**: if it is from here, there will be an answer; if no one knows where it came from, be wary.

That this matters was shown by a real episode: in **August 2025**, at the height of the season, the Guardia Civil's Seprona **impounded lobster imported from France without traceability** bound for the island's restaurants. The tag is not red tape: it is the difference between paying for Menorca and paying for a sign on a wall.

## From the pot to the stew

The most famous destination of this lobster is the **Fornells lobster stew**, which began as a humble fishermen's dish —a bread soup made with whatever went unsold— and ended up the dish that put Menorca on the gastronomic map. We devote a whole article to it: [the Fornells lobster stew](/en/article/fornells-lobster-stew). Here it is enough to recall that a good plate begins long before the pot, in a *nansa* set in the reserve of the north.

## Our view

Menorcan lobster is expensive because it is scarce, seasonal and hard-won, and that is exactly its point. Eat it **between April and August**, ask for the guild's tag and be wary of bargains: a local lobster does not compete on price with an imported one, it competes on truth. That premium does not pay for a whim; it pays for the sea of the north to stay alive.

> A product with a closure and a tag cannot be faked: either it is from here and in season, or it is not. Menorcan lobster is both things or it is nothing.
