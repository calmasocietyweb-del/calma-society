Menorca isn't Ibiza, and that's the charm of it. Here the night is **human-scale**: no mega-clubs, no marquee-DJ circuit, but something more our own —a sunset over the cliff, a cocktail by the harbour, a long-standing club on a summer's small hours—. The party is here, but it's lived **with judgement and without the crowds**. And it almost always starts the same way: watching the sun go down.

What follows are the four ways to live the island's night —the sunset, the beach club, the harbour cocktail bar and the club— and, at the end, **a map of every venue and how to reach each one without having to drive home**.

## The sunset, the main event

In Menorca, the first drink is raised to the sun. The undisputed temple is the **[Cova d'en Xoroi](/en/article/cova-den-xoroi-clifftop-cave)**, in **Cala'n Porter (Alaior)**: a natural cave cut into the cliff that by day and at dusk works as a viewpoint and lounge, and by night, in summer, turns into a club. There's nothing else like it on the island.

The west keeps the best sunsets, because there the sun drops straight into the sea. Beside the **Cap d'Artrutx lighthouse (Ciutadella)**, the **Artrutx Sea Club** brings together sea club, restaurant and cocktail bar facing the water; and on the cliffs of **Cala'n Forcat**, **Coral Menorca** —the venue formerly known as Sa Posta de Sol— keeps the western-sunset tradition alive with food and cocktails. In the north, the sunset is watched from the **Platges de Fornells (Es Mercadal)**, at the **Isabella Beach Club**. The rule is simple: arrive with time to spare, order early and don't rush.

## Beach clubs

For a day that stretches into the night, beach clubs are the option most in tune with calm: a sunbed, a swim, good food and music while the afternoon fades. The most exclusive is **Purobeach Menorca**, in **S'Algar (Sant Lluís)**: the Puro group's first beach club on the island, with a pool, sea access, restaurant and its signature music. Alongside it sit the **Artrutx Sea Club** (west) and the **Isabella Beach Club** (north), both with good food and even better sunsets. Worth booking in high season.

## Harbour cocktail bars

Maó and Ciutadella live facing their harbours, and that's where the best bars are. In the **port of Maó**, **Vida Una** (Moll de Llevant) makes music its backbone —vermouth, wine and cocktails with one of the finest views— and **Contrabandu** is a craft cocktail bar with a speakeasy feel and a terrace, inspired by the 19th-century smuggling trade. In **Cala Blanca (Ciutadella)**, **Jigger** does signature cocktails with a kitchen and a long list of spirits; and **BB Cocktail Bar** offers Mediterranean cocktails with a view in **Binibèquer Vell (Sant Lluís)** and in Maó. These are places for an unhurried drink, not for the rush.

## If you're after a real party

When it's time to dance until late, the night gathers around the harbours. The **port of Ciutadella** is the epicentre: **Jazzbah**, a landmark venue for over twenty years (DJs, themed nights and gigs), and **Kopas Club**, a multi-floor club with terraces and cocktails. In the **port of Maó**, the **Akelarre Jazz & Dance Club** (Moll de Ponent) fills a century-old building with live music and a club. And, again, the **Cova d'en Xoroi** lights up its club session on summer nights.

## The map of the night: where everything is and how to get there

Each plan, in its place on the island. Filter by kind of plan —sunset, beach club, cocktail bar or club—, see where each venue falls and solve the most important part of any night out: **getting home**. Book a chauffeur and forget the car, the parking and who's driving.

## Our take

Menorca's nightlife rewards those who understand it: it isn't quantity, it's place and timing. A sunset on the cliff beats ten clubs, and a good cocktail bar by the harbour is a plan in itself. Go out, enjoy, and above all **leave the car parked**: the best decision on any night is not having to drive home. So when the night is worth it, it's worth arriving —and leaving— with a chauffeur.
