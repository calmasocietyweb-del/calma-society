Menorca is best enjoyed unhurried, and that starts with getting the logistics right. The island is small —barely 50 kilometres end to end— but its treasures are spread out: hard-to-reach southern coves, lighthouses on remote headlands, white villages, talayotic sites and two cities, Maó and Ciutadella, at opposite ends. Knowing how to get around is the difference between a tense trip and a calm one.

This guide sums up your transport options and links to our specific guides: how to get from the airport to your hotel, which excursions to take, how to reach the most beautiful coves, and what the island offers if you arrive by cruise ship.

## The essentials

- **Short distances, tricky access.** You can cross the island in under an hour, but many coves and lighthouses lie at the end of narrow lanes with limited parking.
- **In summer, the car isn't always the best idea.** Between **June and September**, car access to several southern coves is restricted and car parks fill early.
- **Options:** rental car, public bus, taxi and **private minibus with a driver** (transfers and tailor-made excursions).
- **When a private transfer makes sense:** airport to hotel with luggage, for groups, to reach coves with restricted access, or for an excursion without worrying about parking or driving.

## Your options, in brief

- **Rental car.** It gives freedom, but in high season it means getting up early to park and, at some coves, not even that. Book ahead: July and August sell out.
- **Public bus.** Affordable and reasonable between the main towns; less practical for coves and corners, and less frequent off-season.
- **Taxi.** Handy for one-off trips, but expensive for a full day of sightseeing.
- **Private minibus with a driver.** The most comfortable option for groups, families and anyone who wants to truly switch off: they pick you up wherever you are, plan the route with you and handle the rest. Ideal for airport transfers and for excursions to coves, towns, lighthouses and ports.

## Our take

Well-sorted mobility is invisible: when it works, you barely notice it, and the trip flows. Our advice is simple: if you're coming for a few days, in a group, or planning to see the southern coves in high summer, consider a **private minibus** for the key days and forget about maps, car parks and timetables. The luxury here is not driving: gazing out of the window while someone else takes the wheel along the island's dry-stone lanes.
