Menorca ends at its lighthouses. Raised where the land surrenders to the sea, they are the island's most honest viewpoints: no terraces, no noise — just wind, rock and horizon.

## Five lighthouses, five characters

- **Favaritx** (northeast): an almost lunar landscape of black slate. The most dramatic, especially with a rough sea behind.
- **Cap de Cavalleria** (north): the northernmost point, reddish cliffs and immense views.
- **Punta Nati** (northwest): bare stone and solitude, near Ciutadella. The sunset here is unforgettable.
- **Cap d'Artrutx** (southwest): the black-and-white striped tower, perfect for watching the sun fall over the water.
- **Illa de l'Aire** (south): off Punta Prima, the island's tallest, on an islet of black lizards.

## How to experience them

Go at dawn or dusk: the low light turns the rock to gold and spares you the midday heat. Wear comfortable shoes —the ground is uneven— and bring water. And above all, **don't rush**: the point of a lighthouse is to stay and look.

> In winter, with the tramontana wind, the northern lighthouses offer Menorca's purest spectacle: the island alone with the sea.
